## Approach

Adds word-level n-gram/skip-gram context models, targeting natural-language text. The encoder already tracked the last two completed words (prevword, prevword2); this adds a third (prevword3) and four new word contexts:
- **word 4-gram**: the three preceding words plus the word currently being typed.
- **word skip-gram (gap 1)**: the word two back paired with the current word, skipping the immediately preceding word — the biggest contributor.
- **word skip-gram (gap 2)**: the word three back paired with the current word.
Skip-grams capture word dependencies across an intervening word (e.g. 'the ___ of', subject–verb across an adjective), which the contiguous word bigram/trigram miss. Each new model carries its own adaptive counter and bit-history StateMap. All are general language models — no corpus-specific content. Release and debug round-trip gates pass; text files improve most.
Memory: release test (cargo test --release, 4 threads) peaks ~9.1 GB, safe on the 16 GB runner.
