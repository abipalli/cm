## Approach

Deepens the context-mixing network and extends the indirect family. (1) **Indirect order-5/order-6 context models**: the existing order-1..4 indirect family is extended with two longer base contexts, each hashed against the recent history of bytes that followed it; the shared indirect follow-hash is grown from 2^21 to 2^23 slots (FBITS) to cut its collisions on the 384 KB files. (2) **Layer-2 combiner ensemble 5 -> 9**: four more combiners over the layer-1 logits, keyed on orthogonal regimes — high-nibble/opcode-class (binary), character-class (text), nesting depth (source), and the byte-above (2D) — averaged in the logit domain. (3) **Layer-1 specialists 17 -> 24**: seven more specialist mixers selected by coarse, meaningful modes — run-length regime, delta-sign and delta-magnitude (numeric trend), period/record phase, above-char-class, vertical-repeat, and bit-position x match-state. (4) **Retuned the layer-2 learning rate 12 -> 10** for the larger ensemble. SCORE 576366 -> 575771 (-595). RAM-cheap: no context-table growth, only ~160 MB of new mixer/indirect state.

## Iteration notes

The layer-1 specialists were nearly inert against the old 5-combiner ensemble (-33 for four of them) but became productive once the L2 ensemble was widened to 9 (-73, then -152 for the next set) — enriching the combiner layer unlocked the specialist layer, consistent with the known L1<->L2 interaction. L2 rate swept 12->10 (-24..-36) after the structure change; L1 rate stayed optimal at 8. Per-model context-table growth (TBITS 23->25 = -740 locally) was rejected: it OOM-kills the CI runner (verify exit 143) because the parallel round-trip tests commit the full uncompressed tables on Linux. APM-chain expansion, transform contexts (2nd-difference, word-delta, 2D-indirect), record-phase, collision-confirmation checksums, and a 10th/11th combiner were all tried and reverted (neutral-to-negative).
