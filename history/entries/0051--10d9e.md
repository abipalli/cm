## Approach

Opens a new context-model vein by crossing the indirect family (the largest historical win) with the byte-transform families. Each model hashes a cheap byte transform of the recent context together with the rolling history of bytes that have followed that transformed pattern: (1) **high-nibble indirect** — what operand byte follows this opcode-class pattern (the largest single win, -299, on the executable); (2) **byte-delta indirect** and (3) **char-class indirect** — numeric-gradient and letter/digit/space regimes; (4) **low-nibble**, (5) **gap-bigram (c1,c-3)**, (6) **stride-2 (c-2,c-4)**, (7) **stride-3 (c-3,c-6)**, and (8) **wide-gap (c1,c-5)** indirect — sparse/periodic patterns. SCORE 575771 -> 575160 (-611). The five lower-order indirect tables use 2^22 slots (vs 2^23) — they are low-collision so this is ~26 bytes and keeps committed memory near the prior record's footprint.

## Iteration notes

Indirect-on-transform is a genuinely new combination: high-nibble -299, then byte-delta+char-class -179, low-nibble+gap+stride-2 -102, stride-3+wide-gap -57 (clear diminishing returns, stopped at 8). Memory: this adds 8 context models (~+0.27 GB/Cm over the prior record); well within the historical precedent of entry 0012 which added 21 models in one PR. The parallel round-trip test peaks ~8.7 GB locally and all 9 tests pass; the realistic CI peak is ~3 overlapping heavy tests x ~3.67 GB ~= 11 GB, under the 16 GB runner. (Per-model context-table growth via TBITS remains off-limits — it OOMs CI — but adding models is fine.)
